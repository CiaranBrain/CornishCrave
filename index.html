About the pasty

Mining Heritage:

The Cornish pasty is closely tied to Cornwall's mining heritage, where tin and copper mining played a crucial role in the economy. Miners needed a convenient and practical meal that was easy to carry, held up well, and provided a hearty and balanced sustenance during their long, labor-intensive workdays underground.

The Origin:

The origin of the Cornish pasty is deeply rooted in the history and traditions of Cornwall, England. While the precise origin story is somewhat elusive, it is widely believed that the pasty gained prominence in Cornwall during the 17th and 18th centuries, particularly in association with the region's thriving mining industry.

Practical Design:

The pasty's distinctive semi-circular shape and thick crimped edge served practical purposes for miners. It allowed them to hold the crimped edge, avoiding direct contact with the pasty's main body and keeping their hands clean in the mines. Additionally, the crimped edge served as a natural handle, enabling consumption without the use of utensils.

Traditional Cornish Pasty

ingredients

  • 500g (4 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 125g (1/2 cup) unsalted butter, cold and diced
  • 125g (1/2 cup) lard or vegetable shortening, cold and diced
  • Pinch of salt
  • Cold water (about 150ml or as needed)
  • 350g (12 oz) beef skirt, diced
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small rutabaga (swede), peeled and diced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: a knob of butter for each pasty

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pastry: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, diced butter, diced lard, and a pinch of salt. Use your fingertips to rub the fat into the flour until it resembles breadcrumbs. Gradually add cold water, a little at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to make it too wet. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in cling film, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling: In a large bowl, mix together the diced beef, potatoes, onion, and rutabaga. Season the filling with salt and black pepper, ensuring it's well combined.
  3. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F) or 180°C (350°F) for a fan oven.
  4. Assemble the Pasties: Divide the pastry into equal portions, depending on how large you want your pasties. Roll out each portion into a circle of about 20-25 cm (8-10 inches) in diameter. Place a generous portion of the filling on one half of each pastry circle, leaving a border around the edge. Optionally, add a small knob of butter on top of the filling.
  5. Seal the Pasties: Fold the pastry over the filling to create a semi-circle. Press the edges firmly together, and then crimp to seal. Traditional crimping involves folding and pinching the pastry along the edge to create a rope-like pattern.
  6. Bake: Place the pasties on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Beat an egg and brush it over the pasties to give them a golden color when baked. Bake in the preheated oven for about 45-50 minutes or until the pasties are golden brown and cooked through.
  7. Serve: Allow the pasties to cool slightly before serving.
Beef and Stilton Pasty

ingredients

  • 500g (4 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 125g (1/2 cup) unsalted butter, cold and diced
  • 125g (1/2 cup) lard or vegetable shortening, cold and diced
  • Pinch of salt
  • Cold water (about 150ml or as needed)
  • 350g (12 oz) beef skirt, diced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled Stilton cheese
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: a knob of butter for each pasty

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Prepare the Filling: Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the diced beef and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the pan and set it aside.
  3. Saute Vegetables: In the same pan, add a bit more oil if needed. Saute the chopped onions until they turn translucent. Add the minced garlic and sliced mushrooms, and cook until the mushrooms are tender.
  4. Combine Ingredients: Return the cooked beef to the pan with the onions and mushrooms. Stir in the Worcestershire sauce, tomato paste, and season with salt and pepper. Allow the mixture to simmer for a few minutes, then remove it from heat. Let it cool slightly.
  5. Prepare Pastry: Roll out the shortcrust pastry sheets on a floured surface. Using a large circular cutter or plate, cut out circles from the pastry sheets (around 5-6 inches in diameter).
  6. Fill the Pasties: Place a spoonful of the beef and vegetable mixture onto one half of each pastry circle. Sprinkle a generous amount of crumbled Stilton cheese over the filling.
  7. Fold and Seal: Fold the pastry over the filling to create a semi-circle shape. Press the edges firmly together to seal. You can crimp the edges with a fork to ensure they are tightly sealed.
  8. Egg Wash: Place the prepared pasties on the lined baking sheet. Brush the tops of the pasties with the egg wash to give them a golden brown color when baked.
  9. Bake: Put the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 20-25 minutes or until the pasties turn golden brown and crisp.
  10. Serve: Once baked, remove the pasties from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving. Enjoy these delicious Beef and Stilton pasties warm or at room temperature.
